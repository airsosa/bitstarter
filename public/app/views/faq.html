We are not just going to be teaching women and children how to code but taking a very comprehensive approach to developing critical thinking, problem solving, designing, manufacturing, and creating physical objects. We want our participants to become consummate engineers, holding up a piece of device and saying I made this.
It all started with an online course: Startup Engineering taught by Balaji S. Srinivasan at Stanford and also ran on Coursera. The course is regarded as a spiritual sequel to Paypal co-founder Peter Thiel's CS183 course on startups also at Stanford Spring 2012. It bridges the gap between academic computer science and production software engineering. After taking the course, we decided to apply what we learnt to solving a very pressing challenge in our society today and we strongly plan to teach a similar course purposed for a Nigerian audience so that they too can think up solutions and build great businesses.
